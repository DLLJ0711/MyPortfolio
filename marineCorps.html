Honorably Served

August 11, 2002 - Septemper 9, 2014

I served in the nations most powerful fighting force for 12 years. While serving I completed 3 combat tours serving in muliple areas acros Kuwait and Iraq. While serving both foreign and domestically I held multiple billets from secruity, supply chain, logistics and completing my time as a United States Marine Corps Drill Instructor. While I both hated and enjoyed difficult and heart wamring times as an active duty Marine, I have to say earn the of Drill Instructor was was one of my proudest moments. Marines are known to be tough, however, not until I became a Drill Instructor, did I find out what the human body, mind and spirit is actually capable. During my time as an active duty Marine I developed relationships that will last a lifetime and probably longer. I was inspired by and idolized some of the greatest people on earth. I am proud to be a Marine because it defines everythin I stand for I wouldn't change anything if I could. Semper Fidelis.

My awards include: Presidential Unit Citation, 3 Navy & Marine Corps Achievment Medals, Navy Unit Citation, 3 Good Conduct Medals, 6 Iraqi Campaign Medals, Global War On Terrorism Medal

To all who shall see these presents, greetings:

Know Ye, that repsoing special trust and confidence in the fidelity and abilities of David R. McFadden, I do appoint this Marine a Staff Sergeant in the United States Marine Corps, to ranks as such from the First day of February, 2012. Effective with this appointment, you are charged to carefully and diligently execute the duties and responsibilities of a Staff Sergeant of Marines, and I do strictly direct and require all personnel of lesser greade to render obedience to appropriate orders. As a Staff Sergeant of Marines you must set the example for other to emulate. Your conduct and professionalism both on and off duty shall be above reproach. You are responsible for the accomplishment of your assigned mission and for the safety, professional developement and well-being of the Marines in your charge. You will be the embodiment of our institutional core values of honor, courage and commiment. You will lead your Marines with firmness, fairness and diginity while observing and following the orders and directives of you senior leaders and enforcing all regulations and articles governing the disciplince of the Armed Forces of the United States of America. Signed First day of February L. Reynolds, Brigadere General, USMC Commanding
